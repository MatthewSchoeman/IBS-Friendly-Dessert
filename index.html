Part one

The classic bakes

Old‑fashioned recipes where eggs, nuts, and coconut do the structural work — no flour substitutes to second‑guess.

Chocolate

Flourless Chocolate Cake

Rich and fudgy — the eggs and chocolate do all the structural work.

  • Serves 8
  • 25 min bake
  • Oven 190°C / 375°F

Ingredients

  • 200 g dark chocolate (60–70%), chopped
  • 115 g unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp salt

Method

  1. Prep. Preheat the oven to 190°C / 375°F. Grease an 8‑inch (20 cm) round pan and line the base with parchment.
  2. Melt. Melt the chocolate and butter together in short microwave bursts or over simmering water, stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly.
  3. Mix. Whisk the sugar into the chocolate, then beat in the eggs one at a time. Stir in the vanilla and salt, then sift in the cocoa and fold until just combined.
  4. Bake. Pour into the pan and bake about 25 minutes, until the top is set with a thin crust — a toothpick should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.
  5. Cool. Cool in the pan for 10–15 minutes before releasing. It will sink a little in the middle — that’s normal. Dust with cocoa to serve.

Worth knowingIf dairy is also a trigger, swap the butter for a lactose‑free or plant‑based version — it works just as well.

Coconut

Coconut Macaroons

Just egg whites, sugar, and coconut — chewy inside, golden outside.

  • Makes 18
  • 23 min bake
  • Oven 160°C / 325°F

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg whites
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 3 cups shredded unsweetened coconut
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch of salt

Method

  1. Prep. Preheat the oven to 160°C / 325°F and line a baking sheet with parchment.
  2. Make the base. Whisk the egg whites, sugar, and salt until frothy, about 2 minutes. Stir in the vanilla, then fold in the coconut until every shred is coated.
  3. Shape. Scoop heaped tablespoons onto the sheet, squeezing each mound gently so it holds together.
  4. Bake. Bake about 23 minutes, until the tips and edges are deep golden.
  5. Cool. Cool on the sheet for 10 minutes — they firm up as they cool.

Worth knowingCoconut is easiest on sensitive stomachs in modest amounts — 2 to 3 macaroons per sitting is a comfortable serve.

Peanut

Flourless Peanut Butter Cookies

A one‑bowl classic where peanut butter replaces the flour entirely.

  • Makes 12
  • ~20 min total
  • Oven 175°C / 350°F

Ingredients

  • 1 cup natural smooth peanut butter
  • ¾ cup granulated or light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 pinch of salt

Method

  1. Prep. Preheat the oven to 175°C / 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment.
  2. Mix. Stir everything together until a thick, uniform dough forms.
  3. Shape. Roll into 12 balls, space them out on the sheet, and press a criss‑cross into each with a fork.
  4. Bake. Bake about 11 minutes, until the edges are set — the centers will still look soft.
  5. Cool. Cool on the sheet for 10 minutes; they’re fragile straight from the oven and set as they cool.

Worth knowingPeanut butter tends to sit well in servings of around 2 tablespoons — roughly two cookies’ worth.

Part two

The protein five

Quick, low‑calorie, protein‑forward desserts built on Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, and protein powder. Whey isolate or plant‑based powders are gentlest if lactose is an issue.

Chocolate

Chocolate Protein Mousse

Four ingredients, no cooking — whisked fluffy in a couple of minutes.

  • Serves 2
  • 5 min + optional chill
  • No oven
  • ~145 cal · 23 g protein per serving

Ingredients

  • 1 cup nonfat Greek yogurt (lactose‑free if needed)
  • 30 g chocolate protein powder
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp maple syrup (optional, to taste)

Method

  1. Whisk. Whisk the yogurt, protein powder, and cocoa vigorously until completely smooth and slightly fluffy, 1–2 minutes.
  2. Sweeten. Taste, then stir in the maple syrup only if it needs it — many protein powders are sweet enough on their own.
  3. Chill. Chill for 10 minutes to thicken into a proper mousse texture — optional, but worth it. Top with a few strawberries.

Worth knowingWhey isolate or a plant‑based powder is the gentlest choice if lactose is also an issue.

Strawberry

Strawberry Protein Nice Cream

Blender soft‑serve — the frozen fruit does the work, no ice cream maker.

  • Serves 2
  • 2 min blend
  • No oven
  • ~140 cal · 16 g protein per serving

Ingredients

  • 2 cups frozen strawberries
  • ½ cup nonfat Greek yogurt (lactose‑free if needed)
  • 30 g vanilla protein powder
  • 2 tbsp milk of choice

Method

  1. Blend. Blend everything in a food processor or strong blender, scraping down the sides, until it turns into thick soft‑serve, 1–2 minutes. Add an extra splash of milk only if it refuses to turn over.
  2. Serve. Eat straight away as soft‑serve, or freeze for 30–60 minutes if you want it scoopable.

Worth knowingStrawberries are one of the gentlest fruits for sensitive stomachs — blueberries or pineapple work well too.

Chocolate

Flourless Protein Mug Cake

Warm and cake‑like in 90 seconds — zero flour of any kind.

  • Serves 1
  • ~3 min total
  • Microwave
  • ~200 cal · 29 g protein

Ingredients

  • 30 g chocolate protein powder
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp milk of choice
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • 1 pinch of salt

Method

  1. Mix. In a microwave‑safe mug, whisk the egg and milk together, then stir in the protein powder, baking powder, and salt until no dry pockets remain.
  2. Microwave. Microwave on high — start checking at 60 seconds and stop the moment the top looks just set. Overcooking is what makes protein cakes rubbery.
  3. Rest. Let it rest for a minute — it finishes cooking in the mug and stays moist.

Worth knowingA whey–casein blend or plant protein gives the fluffiest crumb — pure whey isolate turns rubbery fastest, so pull it early if that’s what you’re using.

Lemon

No‑Bake Protein Cheesecake Cups

Blended cottage cheese turns completely silky — that’s the whole trick.

  • Serves 2
  • 5 min + 30 min chill
  • No oven
  • ~155 cal · 24 g protein per cup

Ingredients

  • 1 cup low‑fat cottage cheese (lactose‑free if needed)
  • 30 g vanilla protein powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp maple syrup

Method

  1. Blend smooth. Blend the cottage cheese in a food processor or blender until completely silky, 1–2 minutes, scraping down the sides partway through.
  2. Flavor. Add the protein powder, lemon juice, and maple syrup and blend again until fully combined. Taste and adjust — more lemon for tang, a touch more maple for sweetness.
  3. Chill. Divide between two small cups or ramekins and chill 30 minutes, until cold and set. Top with a few berries before serving.

Worth knowingThe long blend is what makes it taste like cheesecake instead of cottage cheese — don’t cut it short. A little lemon zest on top makes it brighter.

Berries

Frozen Yogurt Bark

A two‑ingredient base and five minutes of work — the freezer does the rest.

  • Serves 4
  • 5 min + 3 h freeze
  • No oven
  • ~80 cal · 8 g protein per serving

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups nonfat Greek yogurt (lactose‑free if needed)
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced (or blueberries)
  • 2 tsp maple syrup (optional)
  • 1 tbsp dark chocolate, finely chopped (optional)

Method

  1. Spread. Line a small baking tray with parchment. Stir the maple syrup into the yogurt if using, then spread the yogurt about 1 cm (half an inch) thick.
  2. Top. Scatter the fruit and chocolate over the top, pressing the fruit in lightly so it anchors into the yogurt.
  3. Freeze. Freeze flat for about 3 hours, until completely solid, then snap into shards.
  4. Store. Keep the shards in a freezer bag or container — they last for weeks.

Worth knowingNonfat yogurt melts fast, so eat pieces straight from the freezer. Swirling in a spoonful of the protein mousse turns this into a higher‑protein version.

Every quantity here scales cleanly, so halve or double as needed. Calorie and protein figures are approximate and depend on your brands — and even the friendliest ingredients are happiest in moderate portions.
